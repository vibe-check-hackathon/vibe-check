# Maschmeyer Group / FirstCheck — Seed Term Sheet Template

> **Important:** This is a simplified, non-binding template for demo purposes. It is not legal advice and must be reviewed and customized by qualified counsel before use.

## 1. Parties
- **Company:** _[Company name]_, a _[jurisdiction]_ corporation.
- **Investor:** Maschmeyer Group, investing via **FirstCheck**.

## 2. Financing overview
- **Security:** Seed preferred shares (or SAFE / convertible, as agreed).
- **Investment amount:** **€100,000** (or $100,000) from Maschmeyer Group / FirstCheck.
- **Use of proceeds:** _[Brief description, e.g. product, hiring, go-to-market]_.

## 3. Valuation and ownership
- **Pre-money valuation:** _[To be inserted — determined by FirstCheck based on market, industry, traction, and team assessment]._
- **Post-money valuation:** _[Pre-money + 100k]._
- **Ownership:** _[Percentage]_ fully diluted, calculated on a capitalization table including an employee option pool of _[x]%_ post-closing.

_Add a short note:_
> Valuation is set by FirstCheck using a standard method that considers the target market, competitive landscape, business model, traction to date, and founder profile, rather than only a headline number.

## 4. Liquidation preference
- **Preference:** 1x non-participating preferred.
- On a liquidation event (including sale of substantially all assets or change of control), the Investor receives the greater of:
  - return of the original investment amount, or
  - pro rata share of proceeds on an as-converted basis.

## 5. Dividends
- **Dividends:** Non-cumulative dividends if and when declared by the Board, pari passu with other preferred.

## 6. Conversion
- **Automatic conversion:** All preferred shares convert to common stock:
  - upon a qualified IPO; or
  - upon approval of the preferred majority.
- **Conversion ratio:** 1:1, subject to standard anti-dilution adjustments.

## 7. Pro rata rights *(negotiated by agent)*
- **Template:**
  - Investor has the right, but not the obligation, to participate in future equity financings to maintain its ownership percentage on a fully diluted basis.
  - Pro rata rights apply to priced equity rounds and may be subject to customary exceptions.
- **Negotiation fields (to be filled by negotiation agent):**
  - _[Pro rata percentage / scope]_  
  - _[Exclusions, if any]_  
  - _[Transferability of pro rata right]_  

## 8. Drag-along rights *(negotiated by agent)*
- **Template:**
  - In the event of a company sale approved by:
    - the Board,
    - the preferred majority, and
    - a majority of common stock,
  all shareholders will be required to vote in favor of, and otherwise support, the transaction (drag-along).
- **Negotiation fields (to be filled by negotiation agent):**
  - _[Trigger thresholds]_  
  - _[Minimum price / floor conditions]_  
  - _[Founder protections]_  

## 9. Anti-dilution
- **Protection:** Broad-based weighted average anti-dilution for down rounds.
- **Exceptions:** Standard exclusions (option pool increases, employee grants, approved strategic issuances, etc.).

## 10. Information rights
- Investor to receive:
  - quarterly unaudited financial statements;
  - annual audited financial statements (if available);
  - cap table updates;
  - key metrics and KPIs agreed with the Company.

## 11. Board and governance
- **Board composition at seed:**
  - _[e.g. 2 founder seats, 1 investor seat]_;
  - observer rights for Investor as agreed.
- **Reserved matters:** Certain major actions require approval of the preferred majority (examples):
  - issuing securities senior or pari passu to the existing preferred;
  - changing authorized share capital;
  - redeeming or repurchasing shares (subject to customary exceptions);
  - selling substantially all assets or approving a merger;
  - changing the size of the Board.

## 12. Founder vesting
- Founders’ shares subject to reverse vesting:
  - typical schedule: 4 years with 1-year cliff;
  - specific terms to be agreed.

## 13. Transfer restrictions; tag-along / ROFR
- **Right of first refusal:** Company and/or Investor have a right of first refusal on transfers of founder shares.
- **Tag-along:** Investor may have tag-along rights on certain transfers by founders.
- **Details:** To be set in the shareholders agreement.

## 14. Use of negotiation agent
This term sheet is designed to work with a **FirstCheck negotiation agent**:

- Some fields are pre-set as standard seed terms (1x non-participating liquidation preference, basic information rights, standard anti-dilution).
- Other fields are explicitly left open for negotiation and should be filled programmatically by the negotiation agent at the end of the AI interview and deal process:
  - **Pro rata rights:** scope, exceptions, and transferability.
  - **Drag-along rights:** trigger thresholds, minimum price conditions, founder protections.
  - **Board seat details:** number of investor seats and observer rights.
  - **Option pool size:** post-closing percentage.
  - **Any additional covenants:** e.g. reporting specifics or special consent rights.

Each negotiated field should be stored with:
- the agreed value;
- a summary of the rationale; and
- a link to the transcript excerpt or negotiation event that led to the specific term.

## 15. Conditions to closing
- Completion of satisfactory due diligence.
- Agreement on definitive documents (subscription agreement, shareholders agreement, etc.).
- Any regulatory or third-party approvals (if applicable).

## 16. Exclusivity and confidentiality
- **Exclusivity:** For a period of _[e.g. 30 days]_ after signing this term sheet, the Company will not solicit or enter into discussions with other investors regarding a similar financing.
- **Confidentiality:** Parties agree to keep the terms of this term sheet and related negotiations confidential, subject to standard exceptions.

## 17. Expenses
- Company to cover reasonable legal costs of the Investor up to a cap of _[e.g. €30,000]_, subject to agreement.

## 18. Binding / non-binding
- Non-binding: Commercial terms of the proposed financing are not legally binding and are subject to negotiation and definitive agreements.
- Binding: Confidentiality, exclusivity, governing law, and expenses sections may be binding once the term sheet is signed.

## 19. Governing law
- This term sheet and the definitive agreements will be governed by the laws of _[jurisdiction]_.
