Yesterday, we started building at 11 a.m. and finished this morning at 9 a.m. Two hours later, we received our first term sheet—we got funded.

We built FirstCheck to make this a reality.

FirstCheck is the first autonomous venture fund. We believe there is enormous inefficiency between investors and founders, and we’re building an autonomous system that eliminates that friction.

The first step is sourcing. We combine inbound and outbound signals in a single system. Our agents actively research GitHub, company websites, archives, and other places where founders may be building in stealth. We combine those opportunities with our inbound database to create a unified source for research.

Here, for example, we’re building a company profile for Nimbus AI. Next, our research agents conduct deep, vertical-specific analysis to determine whether the company fits a VC’s investment thesis, which can be customized and modeled within our system.

Our secret sauce is a mathematical model that evaluates an investor’s strategy and the strength of each opportunity’s fit.

We also conduct due diligence by breaking an idea into independently verifiable components. We can outsource parts of that verification process without revealing the founder’s full idea.

Finally, our agents interview the founders to identify gaps or information that our research did not uncover. We use negotiation and game theory to optimize deal terms. We also apply social and behavioral science to model how co-founders interact, understand their personalities, and assess whether they work well together and fit the VC.

The result is a term sheet—and, hopefully, a celebration.

FirstCheck gives VCs a one-stop platform for tracking every opportunity through the investment process. Historical outcomes feed back into the system, allowing it to continuously learn and improve.

For each opportunity, investors can view the pipeline, research, and personality analysis of every co-founder. During the agentic interview, the entire founding team can be interviewed by multiple agents in parallel. On the fly, the system updates its bargaining playbook to estimate the founders’ upper bound and the VC’s target terms.

The system responds to new signals in real time.

The due diligence check produces a score card that informs decision with evidence.
