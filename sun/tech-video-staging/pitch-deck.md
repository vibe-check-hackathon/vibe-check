# Opportunity OS: 60-Second Tech Pitch

## Creative direction

- **Format:** Six slides, 16:9, paced to exactly 60 seconds.
- **Narrative:** Venture decisions run on scattered evidence. Opportunity OS turns
  that evidence into a fast, auditable decision, then into terms.
- **Visual language:** Editorial and evidence-led, not a generic SaaS dashboard.
  Use a warm white canvas, near-black type, signal red for contradictions, and
  electric green for verified evidence. Evidence IDs and connecting lines recur
  across slides so the deck feels like one continuous investigation.
- **Rule:** One idea per slide. Keep all architecture labels large enough to read
  in a video. Animate only to direct attention.

## Slide 1 - The $444B decision bottleneck (0:00-0:08, 8 seconds)

### Content on slide

**Headline:** Venture moves billions on scattered evidence.

**Visual:** A dense field of deck pages, interview snippets, GitHub activity,
market charts, and reference notes rushes toward a single blinking decision:
`INVEST?`. Most evidence misses the decision window.

**Small proof point:** `$444.5B global VC funding in 2025` with a discreet source
label pointing to the academic assessment.

### What draws attention

The large `$444B` resolves into a messy evidence cloud, then the word `INVEST?`
appears alone. The contrast makes the scale-versus-process mismatch immediate.

### Message that lands

Venture capital is a massive market still making high-stakes decisions through
fragmented, manual workflows.

### Talker's track

> Venture moves hundreds of billions, yet the evidence behind each decision is
> still scattered across decks, calls, profiles, and gut feel.

## Slide 2 - The hidden cost is exceptional founders (0:08-0:17, 9 seconds)

### Content on slide

**Headline:** Networks find visibility. We find evidence.

**Visual:** Two founder signals enter from opposite sides. One has a warm intro
and polished profile; the other has product commits, customer proof, and no
network. The traditional funnel admits the first. Opportunity OS illuminates
the evidence around both.

**Three compact failure labels:** `Warm-network bias` / `Weeks of diligence` /
`Opaque terms`

### What draws attention

The cold-start founder initially appears faint, then becomes the most
evidence-rich object on screen.

### Message that lands

The problem is not a lack of promising founders. It is an allocation system
that mistakes visibility and evidence volume for quality.

### Talker's track

> That rewards visibility, misses unconventional founders, and takes weeks to
> turn incomplete evidence into an answer nobody can fully explain.

## Slide 3 - One living Opportunity Card (0:17-0:29, 12 seconds)

### Content on slide

**Headline:** One opportunity. Every claim traceable.

**Visual:** Place a large **Opportunity Card placeholder** at center. Do not lock
in its final UI. Use a neutral frame containing only representative zones:

- Company and founder snapshot
- Founder / Market / Idea-versus-Market axes
- Verified claim, contradiction, and unknown indicators
- Evidence IDs and source links
- Recommendation and draft terms

Around the placeholder, show source fragments connecting to evidence IDs such
as `CLM-003`, `CON-001`, and `GAP-002`. A contradiction changes from hidden to
signal red. Base the placeholder's information hierarchy on
`opportunity-card-example.md`, without reproducing its full content.

### What draws attention

The contradiction is surfaced in real time, then its two conflicting source
lines snap into view. This is the product reveal.

### Message that lands

The Opportunity Card is a persistent, auditable record that distinguishes fact,
inference, contradiction, and unknown instead of flattening them into one score.

### Talker's track

> Our core artifact is the Opportunity Card: a living record where every claim,
> contradiction, unknown, and recommendation links back to evidence.

## Slide 4 - An agentic evidence loop (0:29-0:41, 12 seconds)

### Content on slide

**Headline:** The system investigates what could change the decision.

**Visual:** Keep the Opportunity Card at center while three stages orbit it:

1. `SOURCE` - decks, GitHub, papers, public signals
2. `DEVELOP` - thesis, market, technical, legal, and reference agents
3. `MATCH` - adaptive founder interview and negotiation

Show the system selecting one high-value unknown, routing it to an interview or
research agent, and writing verified evidence back to the card. Under the loop,
show a thin temporal memory line connecting the founder across opportunities.

### What draws attention

Do not animate every component. Animate one question traveling through the loop
and returning as evidence; this makes the architecture feel active rather than
diagrammatic.

### Message that lands

This is not one-shot AI summarization. Specialized agents actively acquire the
most decision-relevant evidence under a time budget, while persistent memory
prevents each opportunity from starting at zero.

### Talker's track

> Specialized agents source, research, and interview in parallel, pursuing the
> missing fact most likely to change the decision and writing it back to memory.

## Slide 5 - From evidence to explainable terms (0:41-0:52, 11 seconds)

### Content on slide

**Headline:** The term sheet becomes an inspectable model.

**Visual:** An Opportunity Card evidence trail flows into three independent
assessment axes, then into a draft term sheet. Highlight one term:

`$100K SAFE · $8M cap`

Open its annotation to reveal evidence inputs, confidence, a deterministic
calculation, and a counterfactual slider: resolving an IP risk changes the
recommended term. Add a small `HUMAN APPROVAL` gate before issuance.

**Research cue:** A subtle bargaining balance references BATNA and multi-issue
trade-offs; do not show an equation in the main presentation.

### What draws attention

The audience sees one assumption change and one term recalculate. This is the
key technical differentiator: terms are generated from inspectable evidence and
models, not invented in prose.

### Message that lands

Opportunity OS joins calibrated claim-level trust, independent assessment axes,
and deterministic term modeling, with final authority retained by the investor.

### Talker's track

> Evidence becomes independent founder, market, and fit assessments, then an
> annotated term sheet whose assumptions can be challenged and recalculated.

## Slide 6 - Compress weeks into 24 hours (0:52-1:00, 8 seconds)

### Content on slide

**Headline:** Discover merit. Decide in 24 hours. Defend every term.

**Visual:** A horizontal timeline compresses from `WEEKS` to `24 HOURS`. End on
three locked outcomes: `Decision-changing insight` / `Traceable recommendation`
/ `Human-approved terms`. The Opportunity Card remains faintly visible behind
them as the system of record.

**Closing lockup:** `OPPORTUNITY OS` followed by `Evidence-backed venture,
end to end.`

### What draws attention

The timeline physically contracts, leaving the 24-hour marker and product name
as the only dominant elements.

### Message that lands

The wedge is evidence-backed founder assessment culminating in an annotated
term sheet: faster access for founders and faster, defensible decisions for VCs.

### Talker's track

> Opportunity OS compresses weeks into 24 hours, so investors discover merit
> earlier, decide faster, and can defend every term.

## Delivery notes

- Aim for 125-140 spoken words per minute; the track is intentionally sparse so
  visuals have room to land.
- Use `opportunity-card-example.md` only as representative content. The visual
  itself must remain a clearly labeled placeholder.
- Avoid claims that personality can be read as fact from facial expressions or
  tone. Frame interview observations as probabilistic evidence tied to specific
  hypotheses, consistent with the business assessment's bias safeguards.
- Keep the investor visibly in control at the decision and legal approval gates.
